# Layered Interface Construction in Web Pedagogy

## Abstract
Layered interface construction helps students separate content, presentation, and behavior in web development courses.

## Introduction
Beginners often mix structural, visual, and behavioral concerns. This makes debugging difficult and weakens their understanding of platform responsibilities.

## Method
The proposed module asks students to implement the same interface in layers: content first, presentation second, behavior third. Each layer is reviewed using a different rubric.

## Experiments
Student projects are analyzed for separation of concerns and graceful fallback behavior. The layered group produces interfaces that remain more usable without JavaScript.

## Discussion
Layering becomes a narrative device across the paper. It begins as a diagnosis of novice confusion, becomes a method for organizing assignments, and later explains fallback behavior.

## Conclusion
Layered construction supports clearer reasoning about web interface resilience.
