# Accessibility Checks as Formative Feedback

## Abstract
This paper studies accessibility checks as formative feedback in web programming assignments.

## Introduction
Accessibility is frequently treated as a final compliance step rather than a design constraint. This sequencing limits students' ability to connect markup decisions with user experience.

## Related Work
Research on computing education suggests that immediate feedback can improve conceptual understanding when it is aligned with learning goals.

## Method
Accessibility checks are embedded after each implementation milestone. Students revise semantic structure, focus order, and labels before adding advanced interaction.

## Experiments
The course compares assignments with final-only checks and milestone-based checks. Milestone feedback leads to fewer repeated accessibility violations.

## Discussion
Accessibility checks are described differently across sections: as a neglected requirement, as an instructional scaffold, and as evidence of improved design reasoning.
