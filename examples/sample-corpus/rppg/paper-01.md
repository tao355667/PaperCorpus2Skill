# Motion-Robust Remote Photoplethysmography

## Abstract
This paper studies motion-robust remote photoplethysmography for non-contact heart rate estimation.

## Introduction
Remote photoplethysmography estimates physiological signals from facial videos, but motion artifacts remain a major challenge. Robust temporal modeling is needed for practical deployment.

## Method
The proposed module suppresses motion-induced noise by combining spatial attention with temporal filtering. The module takes frame-level features as input and produces pulse-related temporal representations.

## Experiments
The method is evaluated on motion-heavy subsets and cross-dataset settings. Ablation studies show that the motion suppression module improves heart rate estimation accuracy.

## Discussion
Motion artifact suppression is introduced as a core limitation, implemented as a module, and validated through ablation and cross-dataset evaluation.
