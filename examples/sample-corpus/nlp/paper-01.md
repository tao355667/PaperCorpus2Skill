# Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive Tasks

## Abstract
This paper studies retrieval-augmented generation for knowledge-intensive question answering.

## Introduction
Large language models can produce fluent answers, but unsupported claims remain a major challenge. Retrieval grounding is needed for practical knowledge-intensive applications.

## Method
The proposed module retrieves relevant passages and conditions generation on external evidence. The module takes a user query as input and produces grounded response representations.

## Experiments
The method is evaluated on open-domain question answering and fact verification benchmarks. Ablation studies show that evidence retrieval improves answer faithfulness.

## Discussion
Retrieval grounding is introduced as a core limitation, implemented as a module, and validated through ablation and cross-dataset evaluation.
